Video Cards / Graphics Cards – CompTIA A+ 220-1201

Two Main Types

Integrated Graphics (onboard)

  • Built into the CPU (or motherboard chipset)
  • Cheap, uses less power, good for normal tasks (web, office, video)
  • Not good for gaming or heavy work

Discrete Graphics (dedicated video card)

  • Separate card plugged into PCIe slot
  • Has own GPU, VRAM, fans, and extra power cables
  • Much faster – used for gaming, editing, 3D, etc.
  • Brands: NVIDIA GeForce, AMD Radeon

How It Connects – PCIe Slots

Use the long **PCIe x16** slot for best performance (gives most bandwidth).

Exam Tip: Plug graphics card into PCIe x16 slot. Plug monitor into the card ports (not motherboard ports) after install!

Common Video Ports / Outputs

  • HDMI → Most common today. Video + audio. Supports 4K, 8K.
  • DisplayPort (DP) → Great for high refresh rates, multi-monitor, daisy-chain.
  • DVI → Older digital (no audio). Still seen sometimes.
  • VGA → Very old analog (blue 15-pin). Low resolution max.
Exam loves this: Know HDMI and DisplayPort are modern digital + audio. VGA is old analog. DVI is digital but no audio.

Quick Exam Cheat Sheet

  • Integrated = built-in CPU, basic use
  • Discrete = PCIe x16 card, powerful, needs power cables
  • Best ports now = HDMI & DisplayPort
  • Old ports = DVI (digital no audio), VGA (analog)
  • Install steps: Power off → insert card → connect power → plug monitor to card → install drivers
  • Troubleshoot: No picture? Check cable/port, power to card, drivers, wrong output used
