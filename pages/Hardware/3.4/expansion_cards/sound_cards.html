What is a Sound Card?

A sound card handles all the sound in/out of the computer: digital to analog for speakers, analog to digital for mics, better quality than basic onboard audio.

Exam Tip: Sound cards are expansion cards in Domain 3.0 Hardware.
Example of a classic PCIe sound card (Creative Sound Blaster)
Modern discrete PCIe sound card with multiple ports

Two Main Types

Integrated Audio (onboard)

  • Built into the motherboard â€“ most common today
  • Good for everyday use (music, videos, calls)
  • Ports on back of motherboard (color-coded)

Discrete Sound Card (dedicated)

  • Separate PCIe card (usually x1 slot)
  • Better quality, more ports, surround sound (5.1/7.1)
  • For gamers, music producers, high-end audio

How It Connects â€“ Expansion Slots

Use **PCIe x1** slot (short one) for sound cards.

Installing a Sound Card (Simple Steps)

  • Power off, unplug PC.
  • Open case, insert into PCIe x1 slot.
  • Secure if needed, close case.
  • Plug audio devices into **new card** ports.
  • Boot â†’ install latest drivers from manufacturer.

Troubleshooting Sound Issues

  • No sound â†’ Check mute/volume, wrong output selected, drivers bad/missing, plugged into wrong ports.
  • Mic issues â†’ Wrong port (pink), settings disabled.
  • Crackling â†’ Bad cable, interference, update drivers.

Quick Exam Cheat Sheet
