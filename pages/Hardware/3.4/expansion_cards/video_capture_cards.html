What is a Video Capture Card?

A video capture card takes video input from devices (like cameras, consoles, old VCRs) and brings it into the computer for recording, streaming, or editing.

  • Captures video (and usually audio) from external sources
  • Used for: live streaming games, digitizing old VHS tapes, video conferences, security cameras
  • Converts analog or digital signals to data the PC can use
Exam Tip: Capture cards are expansion/add-on cards in Domain 3.0 Hardware (same section as sound/video/NIC cards).
Example of an internal PCIe video capture card (HDMI inputs)

Two Main Types

Internal Capture Card

  • Plugs into PCIe slot on motherboard (usually x1 or x4 for lower bandwidth needs)
  • Lower latency, better for high-quality capture (e.g., 1080p60 or higher)
  • Common for desktop PCs and serious streaming/recording

External Capture Card

  • Connects via USB (usually USB 3.0 or higher for speed)
  • Easy plug-and-play, portable, no need to open case
  • Good for laptops or quick setups, but slightly higher latency

Installing a Capture Card (Simple Steps)

  • Power off PC, unplug.
  • For internal: Open case, insert into PCIe slot, secure, close case.
  • For external: Just plug into USB port.
  • Connect video source to input port(s).
  • Boot up â†’ install drivers/software (from manufacturer, like OBS Studio for streaming).
