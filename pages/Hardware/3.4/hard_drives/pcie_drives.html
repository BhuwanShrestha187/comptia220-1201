Power Specifications

Slot Power vs. External Power

  • 75 Watts: Maximum power provided by the motherboard x16 slot alone.
  • 6-pin Connector: Provides an additional 75 Watts (Total 150W).
  • 8-pin Connector: Provides an additional 150 Watts (Total 225W+).
  • PEG (PCI Express Graphics): The name for the auxiliary power cables from the PSU.

Compatibility & Architecture

Backward & Forward Compatibility

  • Physical: You can plug a smaller card (e.g., x1) into a larger slot (e.g., x16). It will work perfectly.
  • Version: A PCIe 4.0 card works in a PCIe 3.0 slot (runs at 3.0 speeds).
  • Bottleneck: The connection will always run at the speed of the slowest component (Slot or Card).

Serial Communication

  • PCIe uses Serial communication (sending data one bit at a time, very fast) rather than Parallel.
  • It is Full Duplex (can send and receive data simultaneously).
  • Dedicated "lanes" mean devices don't have to share bandwidth like older PCI busses.

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Problem Cause Solution
Card not detected Not seated fully, "GPU Sag" Reseat the card, ensure the retention clip clicks, use a bracket.
System unstable/Crashes during gaming Insufficient power Check if 6-pin/8-pin power cables are firmly connected to the GPU.
Slow performance Wrong slot, bandwidth sharing Ensure GPU is in the top x16 slot (closest to CPU) for max speed.
Card physically won't fit Case limitations Check case clearance length; consider a Riser Card for vertical mounting.

  • x16 Slot: Used for GPUs; provides max bandwidth.
  • x1 Slot: Used for Wi-Fi, Sound, USB expansion.
  • Power (Slot): 75 Watts max from motherboard.
  • Power (Aux): 6-pin = 75W extra; 8-pin = 150W extra.

  • Lane Flexibility: Small cards fit in big slots (x1 works in x16).
  • NVMe: Uses PCIe lanes (usually x4) for storage.
  • Duplex: Full Duplex (Simultaneous send/receive).
  • Riser Card: Used to reorient cards for slim cases.
