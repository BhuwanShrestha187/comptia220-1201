What is SAS?

Serial Attached SCSI (SAS) is a point-to-point serial protocol that succeeds the parallel SCSI interface. It is designed for high-performance enterprise environments. SAS drives use the same physical connector as SATA but are electrically different and support advanced features like full-duplex, dual-porting, and larger command queues.

Note: SAS uses 8b/10b encoding (SAS-1/2/3) or 128b/150b (SAS-4). Actual data throughput is roughly 80% of the raw bit rate.

Connectors & Cabling

Internal connectors

  • SFF-8482: Standard for direct-attach SAS drives (backplane connector).
  • SFF-8087: Mini-SAS internal (4 lanes, 36-pin) ‚Äì used for internal backplane connections.
  • SFF-8643: Mini-SAS HD internal (12 Gbps) ‚Äì common for SAS-3.

External connectors

  • SFF-8088: Mini-SAS external (4 lanes) ‚Äì for cabling between enclosures.
  • SFF-8644: Mini-SAS HD external ‚Äì used in high-performance storage.

Backward compatibility: SAS controllers can usually connect to SATA drives (using SATA disks on SAS backplanes), but SATA controllers cannot connect to SAS drives (different protocol).

Advantages of SAS

  • Dual-porting: Two independent data paths for redundancy and load balancing.
  • Full-duplex: Simultaneous read/write.
  • Scalability: Can connect up to 65,535 devices through expanders.
  • Error recovery: Advanced SCSI command set and better error handling.
  • Higher reliability: Designed for 24/7 operation with deeper command queues.
  • Larger drives: Enterprise capacities often higher than consumer SATA.

Important Concepts for A+ Exam

SAS Expanders

Allow connection of many SAS devices to a single controller port (like a switch). Essential for large storage enclosures.

Wide Ports

Aggregating multiple SAS lanes (e.g., 4x) for increased bandwidth.

Multipathing / Dual-port

Each drive has two ports that can connect to different controllers for failover.

Physical size

SAS drives typically 2.5" (10k/15k RPM) or 3.5" (7200 rpm nearline SAS).

Hot-swap

Supported by design ‚Äì drives can be replaced without powering down.

Historical Context & Legacy

  • Parallel SCSI: Predecessor (wide ribbon cables, termination, device IDs).
  • Fibre Channel: Another enterprise alternative, but SAS is more common in direct-attach.
  • NVMe & SSDs: SAS SSDs exist but NVMe is taking over high-performance roles. However, SAS remains strong for spinning disks.

Exam tip: Know that SAS uses SCSI commands, supports higher RPM (15k), dual-port, and is compatible with SATA drives (but not the reverse).
