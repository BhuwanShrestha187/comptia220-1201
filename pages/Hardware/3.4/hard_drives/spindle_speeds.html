CompTIA A+ 220-1101 - RPM Specifications

What is Spindle Speed (RPM)?

RPM = Revolutions Per Minute

The speed at which the hard drive platters spin.

  • Faster RPM = Faster data access
  • Faster RPM = More heat generated
  • Faster RPM = More power consumption
  • Faster RPM = More noise
  • Only applies to HDDs (SSDs have no moving parts)

Common HDD Spindle Speeds

5400 RPM

Most Common Use: Laptop HDDs, budget desktops

  • Speed: Slower
  • Power: Lower power consumption
  • Heat: Runs cooler
  • Noise: Quieter
  • Battery: Better for laptop battery life
  • Best for: Storage drives, media files, laptops

7200 RPM

Most Common Use: Desktop HDDs, gaming PCs

  • Speed: Faster than 5400 RPM
  • Power: Higher power consumption
  • Heat: Runs hotter
  • Noise: Louder
  • Performance: Noticeably faster for OS and applications
  • Best for: Desktop boot drives, general computing

10,000 RPM

Most Common Use: Enterprise servers, workstations

  • Speed: Very fast
  • Form Factor: Usually 2.5" drives
  • Power/Heat: High power and heat
  • Cost: More expensive per GB
  • Capacity: Lower capacities (600GB - 1.8TB typical)
  • Best for: High-performance servers
  • Brand: WD VelociRaptor is common example

15,000 RPM

Most Common Use: Enterprise servers, data centers

  • Speed: Fastest HDD speed
  • Interface: Usually SAS (not SATA)
  • Form Factor: 2.5" drives
  • Power/Heat: Very high power and heat
  • Cost: Very expensive per GB
  • Capacity: Lower capacities (300GB - 900GB typical)
  • Best for: Mission-critical enterprise applications
  • Note: Being replaced by SSDs in modern systems

Important Concepts

Speed vs Capacity Trade-off

  • 5400/7200 RPM: Available in very large capacities (up to 20+ TB)
  • 10,000 RPM: Maximum ~1.8 TB typically
  • 15,000 RPM: Maximum ~900 GB typically
  • Faster speeds = lower capacities available

Form Factor Relationship

  • 3.5" drives: Usually 5400 or 7200 RPM (desktop/storage)
  • 2.5" drives: Usually 5400 RPM (laptops) or 10,000/15,000 RPM (enterprise)
  • Smaller platters can spin faster more easily

Modern Context

SSDs vs HDDs:

  • SSDs have NO spindle speed (no moving parts)
  • SSDs are much faster than even 15,000 RPM HDDs
  • 10,000 and 15,000 RPM drives being phased out
  • For performance, buy SSD; for capacity, buy HDD (5400/7200 RPM)
  • Exam may still test on all RPM speeds

Exam Tips

What to Memorize:

  • 5400 RPM: Laptops, lower power, cooler, quieter
  • 7200 RPM: Desktops, standard performance
  • 10,000 RPM: Enterprise, high performance
  • 15,000 RPM: Enterprise only, fastest HDD
  • Higher RPM = faster but more heat/power/noise
  • SSDs have no RPM (no moving parts)

Common Scenarios

Scenario Best Choice Why
Laptop boot drive SSD or 5400 RPM HDD Low power, better battery life
Desktop boot drive SSD or 7200 RPM HDD Better performance, power not critical
Mass storage (media files) 5400 or 7200 RPM large HDD High capacity, speed less important
Gaming PC 7200 RPM HDD or SSD Fast loading times
Database server SSD or 10,000/15,000 RPM HDD High IOPS, fast access needed
