Hard Disk Drive (HDD) Form Factors

3.5-inch HDD

  • Standard desktop form factor
  • Typically 1‚Äì20 TB capacity
  • Uses SATA interface (SATA III ‚Äì 6 Gbps)
  • Larger platters = higher capacity, lower cost per GB
  • Requires 4-pin Molex or SATA power

2.5-inch HDD

  • Standard laptop / portable form factor
  • Typically 500 GB ‚Äì 5 TB capacity
  • Thinner (7 mm or 9.5 mm height)
  • Same SATA interface as 3.5-inch
  • Lower power, more shock-resistant than 3.5"

Solid-State Drive (SSD) Form Factors

2.5-inch SSD

  • Direct drop-in replacement for 2.5" HDDs
  • Uses SATA III interface (max ~550‚Äì600 MB/s)
  • 7 mm height (fits almost all laptops)
  • Common in budget upgrades and older systems

M.2 SSD

  • Most common modern form factor
  • Sizes: 2230, 2242, 2260, 2280 (most popular), 22110
  • Interfaces: SATA (B+M key) or NVMe/PCIe (M key or B+M)
  • NVMe versions much faster (PCIe 3.0/4.0/5.0)
  • No cables ‚Äì direct slot connection

Key Exam Notes on Form Factors

Must-know facts:

  • 2.5-inch SATA SSDs are backward compatible with any 2.5" HDD bay
  • M.2 SATA SSDs use B+M key ‚Üí work in SATA-only slots
  • M.2 NVMe SSDs use M key (or B+M) ‚Üí require PCIe lanes in slot
  • Always check motherboard/laptop manual for supported M.2 lengths and key types
  • 3.5-inch drives do NOT fit in 2.5-inch bays without adapter/bracket
