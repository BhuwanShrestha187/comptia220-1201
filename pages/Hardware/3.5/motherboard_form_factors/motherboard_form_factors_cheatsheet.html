üß† Memory Aids & Mnemonics

Expansion Slots (7-4-1):
"Seven ATE Four, leaving one" ‚Üí ATX (7), microATX (4), Mini-ITX (1)
Dimensions Pattern:
ATX: Think "12 pack" ‚Üí 12 inches wide
microATX: "Square and fair" ‚Üí 9.6 √ó 9.6 (both same)
Mini-ITX: "Lucky 7" ‚Üí 6.7 √ó 6.7
Power Connectors:
All three use identical connectors: "24 for all" (24-pin main) + "4 or 8 for CPU"
Backwards Compatibility:
"Big cases swallow small boards" ‚Üí Larger cases can fit smaller motherboards

üéØ CompTIA A+ Exam Tips

  • Know exact dimensions: Be able to identify form factors by their measurements (memorize all three)
  • Expansion slot progression: 7 ‚Üí 4 ‚Üí 1 (ATX ‚Üí microATX ‚Üí Mini-ITX)
  • Power connectors are universal: All use 24-pin + 4/8-pin CPU power
  • Case compatibility flows downward: Larger cases accept smaller boards, never the reverse
  • Mini-ITX PSU consideration: May need SFX/smaller PSU for physical fit (not electrical compatibility)
  • ATX = Maximum expansion: Always recommend for multi-card systems
  • microATX = Cost-effective: Best for budget builds with moderate needs
  • Mini-ITX = Space saver: One expansion slot limits use cases
  • Thermal concerns scale down: Smaller = more thermal challenges
  • Remember the years: ATX (1995 by Intel), Mini-ITX (2001 by VIA)

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Tricky Exam Scenarios

Q: Can you use an ATX power supply with a Mini-ITX board?
A: YES, electrically compatible (same connectors), but it may not physically fit in a Mini-ITX case. The question tests if you know the difference between electrical compatibility and physical fit.
Q: A customer needs dual GPUs. Which form factor?
A: ATX only. Mini-ITX has 1 slot (impossible), microATX has 4 slots (but dual GPUs need spacing, usually requiring more slots). ATX's 7 slots is the safe answer.
Q: Which boards fit in a microATX case?
A: microATX and Mini-ITX only. ATX is too large. Remember: cases accept their size and smaller.
Q: Why choose microATX over ATX?
A: Lower cost (smaller PCB) and smaller footprint. Don't say "better performance" ‚Äì that's not form factor dependent.
Q: Customer wants smallest HTPC with full-size GPU. Which form?
A: Mini-ITX. Despite small size, it has one PCIe x16 slot that supports full-length cards (case must have clearance).
