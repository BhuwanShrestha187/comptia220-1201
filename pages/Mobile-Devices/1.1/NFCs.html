1. Which biometric method is currently the most common on modern smartphones?
Correct! Fingerprint readers (capacitive, optical, or ultrasonic) remain the most widespread biometric method on modern smartphones due to reliability, speed, and cost.
2. A user reports their facial recognition stops working in low light. What should be checked first?
Correct! Facial recognition (especially 2D) struggles in poor lighting. Re-training in good lighting usually resolves the issue. 3D systems (Face ID) are more robust but still benefit from proper conditions.
3. What is the maximum effective range of NFC?
Correct! NFC is designed for very short range (typically up to 4 cm) to ensure intentional, secure interactions like payments or tag reading.
4. Which of the following is a primary use of NFC in mobile devices?
Correct! NFC is widely used for secure contactless payments via mobile wallets. It is also used for quick Bluetooth pairing and reading/writing tags.
5. A user cannot use NFC payments after replacing the screen. What is the MOST likely cause?
Correct! The NFC antenna is often integrated into or near the screen assembly. Screen replacement can disconnect or damage it, breaking contactless functionality.
6. Which security feature helps protect biometric data on modern smartphones?
Correct! Modern devices (Apple Secure Enclave, Android TEE) store biometric templates encrypted and never send raw data off-device.
7. What should a user do if fingerprint recognition fails repeatedly?
Correct! Re-registering fingerprints after cleaning the sensor and fingers usually resolves recognition issues.
8. Which statement about NFC is TRUE?
Correct! For secure payments, the device usually needs to be unlocked (PIN/biometric), and NFC range is very short by design for security.
