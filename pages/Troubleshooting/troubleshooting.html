üìù Practice Questions

Question 1: A computer beeps 3 times on startup and displays no video. What is the most likely cause?

Answer: Memory (RAM) error. The 3-beep POST code typically indicates a keyboard or memory error, often caused by unseated or failed RAM.

Question 2: A user reports their smartphone battery has visibly swollen and is pushing the screen outward. What should you advise?

Answer: Stop using the device immediately. Do not charge it. The swollen lithium battery is a safety hazard and should be replaced by a qualified technician. Dispose of properly at a recycling center.

Question 3: A laser printer is producing faint prints with white streaks. What is the most likely cause?

Answer: Low toner or a dirty/worn drum unit. Try replacing the toner cartridge first. If the issue persists, the drum unit likely needs replacement.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • POST beep codes: 1=success, 2=memory error, 3=keyboard error, continuous=RAM issue
  • Storage: Clicking = imminent failure, back up immediately, replace drive
  • Mobile: Swollen battery = safety hazard, stop using and replace professionally
  • Network: Start with physical layer, then IP configuration, then application
  • Printers: Paper jams = check for fragments, faded prints = check toner/drum
